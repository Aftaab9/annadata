--- SLIDE 1 · Title ---

Good morning. We are Group 3 — Aftaab, Isaac, and Ishaan — presenting Annadata,
also known as AgroSight. This deck is a workflow walkthrough of our live farmer
prototype: how a rural processor moves from a phone photo to a grade, a fair
mandi price, a yield simulation, and a market listing — with humans still in
the loop. The live app is at annadata-lake.vercel.app; we also run it locally
on port 5173 for demos.

--- SLIDE 2 · Agenda ---

Here is the path we will follow. We start with why we built two separate
computer-vision models, then walk screen-by-screen through Inspect, Yield,
Prices, Market, Advisory, Insights, and Ethics. Every process step you see
uses a real screenshot from the app — not a mockup.

--- SLIDE 3 · Why two CV jobs ---

Leaf health and produce quality are not the same task. On the left, Leaf mode
looks at on-plant disease — Healthy, Surface Defect, or Blight/Mold — trained
on PlantVillage with EfficientNetB0 at 97.4% hold-out accuracy. On the right,
Produce mode judges post-harvest spoilage — Fresh, Borderline, or Spoiled —
where ResNet50 won our bake-off at roughly 97% accuracy and 97.1% F1. One
Inspect screen, two purpose-built models.

--- SLIDE 4 · Landing ---

This is the landing hub. From here the operator picks Inspect, Yield, Prices,
Market, Advisory, Insights, or Ethics. The design goal is one PWA that a small
rural line can run on a phone or laptop without switching tools.

--- SLIDE 5 · Leaf upload ---

In Leaf mode the user uploads or captures a leaf photo and runs inference
in-browser via TFLite. No server round-trip — important for flaky rural
connectivity. The model compares five architectures; EfficientNetB0 is the
winner at 97.4%.

--- SLIDE 6 · Leaf outcome ---

The model returns one of three classes. HEALTHY means no actionable disease.
SURFACE_DEFECT is early or cosmetic stress — monitor and adjust handling.
BLIGHT_MOLD is active disease — the app shows treatment advice and flags higher
yield risk. You can see the result card and treatment tips in this screenshot.

--- SLIDE 7 · Leaf → Yield ---

The leaf result does not stop at advice. A disease classification maps to a
defect rate that auto-fills the Yield simulator — shown here — so the operator
does not re-type numbers. That is how field inspection connects to line
throughput planning.

--- SLIDE 8 · Produce upload ---

Switch to Produce mode for post-harvest batches. Same Inspect UX, different
model: ResNet50 beat MobileNetV2 and EfficientNetB0 on our hold-out set at
about 97% accuracy and 97.1% F1.

--- SLIDE 9 · Fresh outcome ---

A Fresh classification maps to Grade A or B. The fair-price rule applies a
premium over the mandi baseline. Grade and defect percentage also flow forward
to Yield and Market automatically.

--- SLIDE 10 · Spoiled outcome ---

Spoiled or borderline quality maps to Grade C with a discount — honest grading
so buyers trust the Grade Card. If confidence drops below 70%, HITL kicks in:
the supervisor must confirm before listing on the market.

--- SLIDE 11 · Next actions ---

After produce inspect, four one-click paths open: Price for mandi fair ₹,
Yield to simulate the line, Market to list with a Grade Card, or Assistant for
crop and fertilizer advice. No copy-paste between modules.

--- SLIDE 12 · Yield simulator ---

The Yield page takes seven process parameters — moisture, RPM, temperature,
grade, defect rate, batch size, line speed — and predicts yield percentage and
throughput in kg per hour. CV auto-fills defect and grade from Inspect. Our
hold-out yield MAPE is 3.52%.

--- SLIDE 13 · Optimal scenario ---

We run Stressed, Baseline, and Optimal scenarios. One click on Optimal applies
a playbook that flags the bottleneck and lifts throughput. Our beans pilot
validated at 94.2% against line expectations.

--- SLIDE 14 · Mandi prices ---

The Prices screen links live mandi reference data to the same Grade A/B/C
language from Inspect. Premium or discount is applied consistently. Price
forecast MAPE on hold-out is 3.4%.

--- SLIDE 15 · Market ---

On Market, the seller lists a lot with a Grade Card — grade, confidence, fair
price badge — so buyers see machine-assisted quality, not a vague description.
This closes the loop from photo to trusted listing.

--- SLIDE 16 · Crop advisory ---

Advisory takes soil N, P, K, pH, rainfall, and temperature and recommends the
best crop among six Annadata SKUs. The random forest exports to ONNX for
in-browser inference at 96.74% hold-out accuracy.

--- SLIDE 17 · Fertilizer advisory ---

Given crop and soil inputs, the fertilizer model suggests a mix for the same
six SKUs. We report 84.44% hold-out accuracy honestly — it is advisory guidance,
not a legal prescription.

--- SLIDE 18 · Insights KPIs ---

The Insights dashboard summarizes session KPIs alongside our measured hold-out
numbers: 97.4% leaf, ~97% produce, 96.74% crop, 84.44% fertilizer, 3.52% yield
MAPE, 3.4% price MAPE. These are the same figures in our Colab training runs.

--- SLIDE 19 · Insights charts ---

Insights also embeds training charts — model comparisons and confusion matrices
— so faculty and operators can audit how each model was selected, not just see
a headline accuracy.

--- SLIDE 20 · Leaf training charts ---

Here is the PlantVillage bake-off: five architectures compared, EfficientNetB0
on top at 97.4%, with the hold-out confusion matrix beside it. SKU-level
breakdown is in our charts folder as well.

--- SLIDE 21 · Produce training charts ---

Produce quality shows the same rigour: ResNet50 wins over MobileNetV2 and
EfficientNetB0, with the confusion matrix confirming strong Fresh vs Spoiled
separation at 97.1% F1.

--- SLIDE 22 · Yield & tabular training ---

Yield regression model comparison and SHAP feature importance explain which
process inputs drive throughput. Below that, tabular model comparison and
confusion matrix cover crop and fertilizer RF models — 96.74% and 84.44%
respectively.

--- SLIDE 23 · Ethics ---

Ethics is not an appendix. When confidence is below 70%, the app blocks market
listing until a supervisor reviews. We frame the business case as less waste in
rupees, not payroll cuts — sorters become validators and negotiators, not
replaced workers.

--- SLIDE 24 · End-to-end summary ---

Pulling it together: inspect leaf for disease advice, inspect produce for grade,
link grade to mandi price, simulate yield with CV-filled defects, list on market
with a Grade Card, get crop and fertilizer advice, and always keep HITL in the
loop. Beans pilot at 94.2% validates the yield line on real data.

--- SLIDE 25 · Close / Q&A ---

That is Annadata — inspect honestly, price fairly, optimize the line, keep
humans in the loop. We are happy to take questions or do a live demo at
annadata-lake.vercel.app. Thank you.
